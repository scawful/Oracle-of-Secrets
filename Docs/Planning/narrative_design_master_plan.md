### Ganondorf Boss Fight (Core Scope)

**Form:** Humanoid Ganondorf (wizard-king, not Pig Ganon)
**Arena:** Floating platform over lava, portals on edges (falling = damage/reset)
**Phase Transitions:** Health thresholds (Phase 2 at 66% HP, Phase 3 at 33% HP)

#### Three-Phase Pattern

| Phase | HP Range | Ganondorf's Action | Link's Opening |
|-------|----------|-------------------|----------------|
| 1 | 100-66% | Summons portal energy (seals flicker) | Master Sword strikes when golden light breaks |
| 2 | 66-33% | Recovers, channels more power | Stun with Light Arrows, then sword combo |
| 3 | 33-0% | Desperate final summon (ground shakes) | Full vulnerability, killing blow |

**Visual Cues:**
- Seal flickers: Three triangles appear around Ganondorf, pulsing
- Portal energy: Swirling darkness drawn from the rift
- Vulnerability windows: Golden light briefly breaks through

## Core Concept

The Sea Zora vs River Zora conflict was **deliberately engineered by Kydrog** as part of his invasion strategy. A divided Zora populace cannot coordinate to seal the dimensional rifts he uses. The conflict resolves in **multiple stages**:

1. **D4 (Zora Temple):** Princess's dying revelation via Song of Healing
2. **Post-D6:** Access to East Kalyxo region (Hammer required)
3. **East Kalyxo:** Full reconciliation at River Zora Village

---

## Kydrog's Manipulation: The Conspiracy Layers

| Layer | What Happened | Evidence |
|-------|---------------|----------|
| **Forged Diplomacy** | Kydrog's agents intercepted and rewrote messages between Zora courts | Letters in temple archives |
| **False Flag Raids** | Pirates wore stolen/manufactured River Zora armor to attack Sea Zora settlements | The armor itself, hidden in temple |
| **Sabotaged Research** | Portal-sealing project destroyed, River Zoras blamed | Research logs showing external tampering |
| **Silenced Witnesses** | Anyone who saw the truth was imprisoned or killed | The Princess herself |

**Why the Zoras?**
- Crystal-mirror magic threatens Kydrog's portal access
- United Zoras could seal the rifts
- Temple research was developing counter-measures
- Division prevents coordinated response

## Glacia Estate & Twinrova (D5 Lore)

### The Estate's History

**Era 1: Golden Age (Age of Portals)**
- Glacia family = Kalyxian nobles wealthy from crystal trade
- Estate built near natural portal site (thin Abyss boundary)
- Crystals from this area were potent for Zora mirror-magic

**Era 2: Decline (Age of Occupation)**
- Hylian occupation disrupted trade routes
- Gorons retreated, Zoras became isolationist
- Glacia family's wealth dried up
- In desperation, they tried to extract power from the Abyss

**Era 3: The Fall**
- Experiments succeeded too well - cold seeped through
- Estate froze from within, servants became ice statues
- Family consumed by greed, frozen in time
- Fire Rod was their last defense against the cold

**Era 4: Twinrova Arrives (Present)**
- Discovered the portal site
- Need to stabilize rift for Ganondorf's escape
- Frozen estate provides perfect cover

### Fire Rod Location
**Frozen Treasury** - A vault of frozen riches where one item still glows with warmth. The Glacia family's most precious defense, now Link's tool.

### Post-D5: Lava Lands Portal

**Map 0x07** - East of the estate, accessible immediately after defeating Twinrova.


## Ganondorf: Final Boss Design

**Form:** Humanoid Ganondorf (wizard-king, not Pig Ganon)
**Location:** Lava Lands (heart of Eon Abyss)
**Weapon Requirement:** Master Sword only

### Three-Phase Pattern

| Phase | Ganondorf's Action | Link's Opening | Attack |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|--------|
| 1 | Summons portal energy (seals flicker) | Brief vulnerability | Master Sword strikes |
| 2 | Recovers, channels more power | Stun with Light Arrows | Sword combo |
| 3 | Desperate final summon (ground shakes) | Full vulnerability | Killing blow |

### Visual Cues
- **Seal flickers:** Three triangles appear around Ganondorf, pulsing
